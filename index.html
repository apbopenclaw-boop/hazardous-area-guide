§ 02 — Why this exists

Hazardous area equipment selection shouldn't require a sales call.

Selecting ATEX-certified communication equipment for explosive atmospheres is a safety-critical decision. But finding reliable, comparable specifications is unreasonably difficult. Manufacturer datasheets use inconsistent terminology, certification details are buried in PDF appendices, and most "comparison" sites are thinly-disguised reseller pages.

This guide network exists to fix that. Every specification is verified against the manufacturer's Certificate of Conformity or IECEx online records. Every device is presented in the same comparable layout. No paid placements. No vendor partnerships. No affiliate commissions.

Principle 01

Vendor-neutral.

No commercial relationships with any equipment manufacturer. Scores are based on published specifications and certification data, not advertising budgets.

Principle 02

Certificate-verified.

Every Ex notation, zone rating, gas group, and temperature class is checked against the actual EU-Type Examination Certificate or IECEx Certificate of Conformity.

Principle 03

Written for engineers.

These guides are written for HSE engineers, procurement teams, and operations managers — not for consumers. The tone is technical. The scope is practical. The goal is faster, safer procurement decisions.

§ 03 — Standards quick reference

The standards behind every certification.

ATEX

Directive 2014/34/EU

The EU directive governing equipment and protective systems intended for use in potentially explosive atmospheres. Mandatory for equipment placed on the EU/EEA market. Requires CE marking and a Notified Body-issued EU-Type Examination Certificate.

Scope: EU/EEA + Switzerland, Turkey, and countries accepting CE marking.
IECEx

IEC 60079 series

The international certification scheme for explosive atmospheres, administered by the IEC. Accepted in 30+ countries including UK, Australia, Singapore, UAE, Saudi Arabia, Brazil, and South Africa. Based on the same IEC 60079 technical standards as ATEX.

Scope: Global (voluntary). Increasingly required alongside or instead of national schemes.
Worker protection

Directive 1999/92/EC

The "ATEX Worker Protection Directive" — also known as ATEX 153 (formerly ATEX 137). Requires employers to classify hazardous areas into zones, select appropriate equipment, and implement an Explosion Protection Document. Transposed into national law as DSEAR (UK), VEXAT (Austria), etc.

Key obligation: The employer — not the equipment manufacturer — is responsible for ensuring the correct zone classification and equipment selection.
